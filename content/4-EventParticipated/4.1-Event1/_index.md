# Report on “FCAJ Community Day 23/5”

### Event Objectives

- Sharing the latest technology trends in Artificial Intelligence (Generative AI) and Cloud Computing
- Introducing methods for managing personal knowledge to improve learning and work efficiency
- Providing knowledge about modern Cloud infrastructure and the role of CloudFront in optimizing performance and security
- Sharing practical applications of AI in businesses, along with product development experiences from Hackathon competitions

### Speaker List

- **Tinh Truong** - Build Second Brain
- **Thinh Nguyen** - From Edge To Origin: CloudFront as Your Foundation.
- **Dao Duc** - Deep Dive on Large Language Models (LLMs)
- **Cat Vy** - Enterprise Multi-Agent System
- **Hai Anh** - Friendly AI Assistant with Amazon Quick
- **Team VIB** - AI Applications & Hackathon Experience

### Featured Content

#### Build a Second Brain - Managing Personal Knowledge

- Introducing the method of building a **Second Brain** to systematize information and knowledge.
- Increasing the ability to remember, reuse knowledge, and improve work productivity.
- Helping to free up thinking, focus on creativity instead of manual memorization.

#### From Edge to Origin - CloudFront as Your Foundation

- Explaining the role of **Amazon CloudFront** in modern cloud architecture.
- Optimizing access speed through the **Edge Locations** system.
- Reducing latency, increasing performance, and enhancing application security.

#### Deep Dive on Large Language Models (LLMs)

- Explains the operating principles of **Large Language Models**.
- Analyzes how LLMs process data, generate language, and create responses.
- Helps understand the technical foundation behind current Generative AI applications.

#### Enterprise Multi-Agent System

- Introduces the **Multi-Agent System** architecture in an enterprise environment.
- Illustrates a practical application through a credit scoring problem for startups.
- Divides multiple AI agents into independent tasks, coordinating to deliver more accurate results.
- Demonstrates scalability and automation in complex business processes.

#### AI Applications & Hackathon Experience

- Introduces the **Friendly AI Assistant** built with Amazon Q to assist users in interacting with data.
- Sharing the journey of developing the **UTMorpho** project in 36 hours at the LotusHacks competition.
- Providing a practical perspective on the product development process from idea to complete demo under time pressure.

### What I Learned

#### Design Thinking

- **Knowledge Management**: Understanding the importance of building a personal knowledge management system to improve long-term work performance.
- **Business-first approach**: AI technology needs to stem from the real needs of businesses instead of focusing solely on technical aspects.
- Always combining Cloud and AI platforms to create solutions with practical value.

#### Engineering Architecture

- Understanding the role of **Amazon CloudFront** in optimizing system performance and security.
- Understanding the operating principles of **Large Language Models (LLMs)**.
- Understand how **Multi-Agent Systems** collaborate to solve complex business problems.
- Recognize the connection between AI, Cloud, and modern system architecture.

### Applications in Work

- **Apply the Build Second Brain method** to manage learning materials and technical documents during the learning process.
- **Research and implement Amazon CloudFront** for web applications to improve access speed and user experience.
- **Learn and develop an AI Assistant** to support document retrieval, data analysis, and automation of certain tasks.
- **Research the Multi-Agent model** for data analysis or decision support problems in businesses.
- Apply experience from the Hackathon to planning and developing personal projects in a short timeframe.

### Event Experience

Participating in **FCAJ Community Day on May 23rd** was a very rewarding experience, giving me a comprehensive understanding of the combination of **Cloud Computing, Generative AI**, and its practical applications in businesses. Some highlights included:

#### Learning from Experienced Speakers
- Speakers shared a wealth of practical knowledge about **CloudFront, LLMs, Multi-Agent Systems**, and the **Build Second Brain** methodology.
- The presentations focused not only on theory but also included many real-world business examples.

#### Experiencing Modern AI Technologies
- Gaining a better understanding of the workings of **Large Language Models** and how AI generates responses.
- Approaching the **Enterprise Multi-Agent System** model, thereby visualizing how multiple AI agents collaborate to handle complex business processes.
- Learn how to build an **AI Assistant** to help users interact with data more effectively.

#### Networking and Exchange
- The event provided an opportunity to meet experts, engineers, and others interested in AI and Cloud.
- Through the exchange sessions, I gained a better understanding of the development trends of Generative AI and the skills needed to work in the Cloud and AI fields.

#### Lessons Learned
- AI will increasingly play a crucial role in software development and digital transformation for businesses.
- A well-designed Cloud system with services like **Amazon CloudFront** is the foundation for applications to achieve high performance and scalability.
- Building a **Second Brain**, combined with AI and modern tools, will help improve learning and work productivity in the long term.
- Practical experiences from the Hackathon showed that problem-solving skills, teamwork, and rapid deployment are crucial elements for successful product development.
